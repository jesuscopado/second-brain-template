## Overview

Huxley's dystopia of total comfort. No Big Brother needed — people control themselves through pleasure. Soma (the perfect drug), engineered happiness, unlimited entertainment. Nobody rebels because nobody suffers. And nobody suffers because nobody strives.

## Key ideas

- **Control through pleasure**: Orwell feared we'd be destroyed by what we fear. Huxley feared we'd be destroyed by what we love. Comfort, entertainment, and instant gratification as tools of control.
- **The abolition of struggle**: Citizens are engineered for their role, conditioned to love their servitude. No friction, no effort, no growth. The ultimate [[The Meaning Is in the Struggle|surrogate existence]].
- **Soma as metaphor**: A drug that eliminates all discomfort with zero side effects. Social media, AI assistants, streaming — each one a micro-dose of soma.
- **Savage vs. World State**: John the Savage demands the right to suffer, to age, to fail. The Controller can't understand why anyone would choose pain. That's the whole argument.

## Highlights
